 This Site consists of some Pages and some topics, related to your lives and my life, your and  my circle of spiritual and practical interests. Because our native language is Russian, I tried to make most of the Pages bilingual, in English and in Russian. 

In MY Home Page, I introduce myself in  Autobiography. Besides, you can read the articles about  Jewish Internet and, I hope, know more about   presence of Jewish related sites on Internet. 

My mother was a journalist, and she has been writing poems all her long life. My big pleasure is to introduce some of them on her Page. 

One of our big losses in emigration is breaking off our former live contacts with great Russian literature. Followed my own liking, I selected -- for you and me -- the Library from Russian newspapers, magazines, and books placed on the Internet. From time to time, I will add and change contents of  the library's bookshelves saving all the former links in the Main Catalog.

Israel is the country, news from which is always interesting  to us. On the  Israeli Page, I will try to place the sort of Israeli news that could be useful for future immigrants and visitors.

On the Legal Pages, I will place such important information for our immigrants as Citizenship exam, Green Card lotteries, etc.

On the ESL Page I am going to publish  English Grammar  materials from my boxes. I compiled them from some ESL textbooks when I was an ESL student, and I hope these lists would be helpful to you.


At last, I have established the Hot Links For You.

  ESL (English as a Second Language) Page (1): Tables of Tenses 
 

I hesitate opening this Page. The matter is that being an ESL student (and after), I gathered from different textbooks large number of materials about American English Grammar -- Tenses, Preposition Combinations, Idioms, etc., more than 300 pages typewriting. Part of them was put into my computer. All of those was done for myself and needs of my own. Nevertheless, I've thought recently, maybe the main lists would be helpful for you. So, I take a start. After that you will decide whether or not the continuation has a sense.
